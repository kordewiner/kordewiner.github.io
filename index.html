What is "Kordewiner"?

My rare surname "Kordewiner" is the Middle High German word of the English Cordwainer and the French Cordonnier - alternate German spellings in the middle ages were "Kurdewaner" or "Korduwan". Nowadays there is only the word "shoemaker" in the German ("Schoster" in Plattdüütsch and "Schuster" or "Schuhmacher" in Hochdeutsch) and the Dutch ("Schoenmaker") language, because most 'French sounds' and lookalikes in German were eliminated in the 19th century, following the Révolution française and the Napoleonic Wars.

But in the Netherlands the surname Kordewiner / Cordewiener / etc. survived - maybe this is special to the former Fench Département de la Meuse-Inférieure (1795-1804) and/or the Meuse–Rhine Euroregion from nowadays. Many of my ancestors come from the southeast of the province Limburg of the Netherlands and from the district of Aachen in Germany. Some of them worked as coal miner and Steiger in the 19th and 20th century in the "Oostelijke Mijnstreek" (NL) and "Wurmrevier" (D) on both sides of the border in e.g. Kerkrade (where my grandparents Anna Maria and Heinrich married) and Eygelshoven (where my father Leonhard "Leo" Wilhelm Maria was born).
